The Issue

A magazine, and the collection it gathers around itself

Exposition is the flagship annual business magazine of the Department of Industrial Management, University of Kelaniya — written, edited and produced by its undergraduates for more than two decades.

Each issue bridges management and information technology: interviews with the industry leaders shaping Sri Lanka's economy, research and reflection from academics, and the working knowledge of an alumni network spread across the world's technology and consulting firms.

Issue 22 arrives the way every issue arrives — at the end of a season of events. A podcast series, a university tech-events hub, an industrial forum and a career fair each gather their own audience, and all of them resolve in a single evening: the launch of the printed magazine.

01
Podcast Series · Minds of Tomorrow

The conversation begins off the page

Before a word of Issue 22 is printed, its themes are spoken aloud. The podcast series sits industry leaders, academics and alumni in front of a microphone for long-form conversations about the ideas the magazine will carry.

Each episode is a working draft of the issue itself — the questions asked here become the interviews, columns and debates that reach print at the launch.

02
Uni-Tech Hub · One Network

Every campus tech event, connected

The Uni-Tech Hub gathers the technology events of Sri Lanka's universities into a single map — hackathons, workshops, meet-ups and competitions that once lived on separate noticeboards.

For students it is one place to find what is happening; for organisers, a shared audience. The hub keeps the network alive between issues, so the magazine never starts from silence.

03
Industrial Forum · Industry Meets Academia

One table, two worlds

The Industrial Forum seats executives, engineers and faculty around the same table to argue the questions that matter to both: what industry needs from graduates, and what research should ask of industry.

The forum's conclusions are not filed away — they are written up, edited and carried into Issue 22 as its analytical spine.

04
Career Fair · First Doors

The key changes hands

The career fair brings the department's partner companies to campus for a day of interviews, portfolio reviews and offers — the moment the magazine's readership steps into its subject matter.

For many undergraduates it is the first professional door that opens; for partners, first access to a cohort trained at the intersection of management and IT.

05
Expo Next 10 · The Decade Ahead

Ten stars for the next ten years

EXPO NEXT 10 turns the collection's attention forward: a stage for the ten ideas, ventures and voices most likely to define the coming decade of Sri Lankan business and technology.

It is the closing argument of the season — and the reason the launch that follows feels less like an ending than a beginning.
